## Connection 1 — SST Geometry-Over-Ontology (CITE NOW)

The Frozen Kernel imposes behavioral geometry — directional state machine,
containment relationships, sequencing — rather than an exhaustive ontology
of prohibited behaviors. Burgess’s critique of RDF/OWL in the Semantic
Spacetime music collection article maps directly onto the Frozen Kernel’s
critique of RLHF rule enumeration: both argue that exhaustive rule
enumeration is brittle, and that geometric constraint is the correct
engineering response.

## Connection 2 — Promise Theory Unilateral Architecture (VERIFIED)

**Verification date:** 2026-03-18
**Sources confirmed:** Primary sources read and cited below.

Hard constraints as architectural promises the model cannot override because
it never made them. Burgess’s foundational tenet: no agent may make promises
on behalf of another. An imposition — an external instruction to an agent —
is not a promise because it cannot be kept by the agent who issues it. The
Frozen Kernel makes promises on behalf of the architecture, not the model.
The model receives an imposition (the boot instruction); the architecture
makes the promise (the constraint holds). These are structurally distinct
in Promise Theory and that distinction is the Frozen Kernel’s core claim.

**Key primary source quote (Burgess, Promise Theory):**
“Autonomous implies the assumption that agents cannot be coerced into making
promises, hence no agent may make promises on behalf of another.”

“Impositions are not promises because they cannot be kept by the agent
who makes them.”

**Primary source (verified):**
Burgess, M. & Fagernes, S. (2006). “Promise Theory — A model of autonomous
objects for pervasive computing and swarms.” International Conference on
Networking and Services (ICNS’06). ISBN 0-7695-2622-5.

Note: Working session originally cited 2007 — corrected to 2006 per
